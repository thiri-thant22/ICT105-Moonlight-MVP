# Problem Notes

## Selected Case

Budget Tracker App for University students and young professionals who have difficulty managing their expenses.

## Target Respondents

| Respondent Type | Why This Respondent Matters | Planned Number | Actual Number |
|---|---|---|---|
| University students and young professionals | They are the target users who frequently manage personal expenses | 10 | 10 |

## Observed / Reported Problem

Many University students do not track their expenses regularly, often rely on memory, and frequently spend more than they planned.

## Current Alternatives or Workarounds

| Workaround | Who Uses It? | Weakness / Pain | Evidence Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Memory only | Most respondents | Easy to forget spending details | Interview |
| Notebook | Some respondents | Not convenient to update regularly | Interview |
| Phone Notes | Some respondents | Difficult to organize spending records | Interview |
| Excel Spreadsheet | Some respondents | Takes time and effort to maintain | Interview |

## Repeated Pain Points

| Pain Point | Number of Mentions | Example Evidence | Severity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cannot remember expenses | 8/10 | Respondents rely on memory and forget where money was spent | High |
| Overspending | 7/10 | Respondents exceed their monthly budget | High |
| No budgeting app usage | Most respondents | Use notebooks, notes, or Excel instead | Medium |

## Consequences

| Consequence | Example | Evidence Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Poor financial awareness | Users cannot clearly see where money goes | Moderate |
| Budget overruns | Spending exceeds monthly plan | Moderate |
| Inefficient expense tracking | Users use manual methods instead of dedicated apps | Moderate |

## Notes for MVP Direction

The MVP should focus on simple expense recording, spending summaries, and budget tracking. The app should be easier and faster to use than notebooks, notes, or Excel.
