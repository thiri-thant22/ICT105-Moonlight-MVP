# Lab 04 - User Persona

> Replace the practice example with your own group case.

## 1. Persona Name
Kelly, 20-year-old University Student

## 2. User Type
University student who wants to manage daily expenses and avoid overspending.

## 3. Background and Context
- Kelly is a second-year university student who receives a monthly allowance from her family.
- She experiences budgeting problems when paying for food, transportation, shopping, and school-related expenses.
- The problem usually happens near the end of the month when she realizes she has spent more than expected.
- She currently uses her phone's Notes app and sometimes relies on memory to track expenses.

## 4. Goals
- Track daily expenses easily.
- Stay within her monthly budget.
- Know where her money is spent.
- Improve her saving habits.
- View a simple summary of her spending.

## 5. Pain Points from Lab 03 Evidence

| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Frequently forgets to record expenses | R001, R003, R005 | Users often forget to update their expense records after spending money. |
| Often overspends | R001, R005, R007 | Users spend more than their planned budget. |
| Cannot identify spending categories | R002, R006 | Users do not know which category uses most of their money. |
| Manual tracking is inconvenient | R004, R008 | Recording expenses in notebooks or spreadsheets takes too much time. |

## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds

| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Notebook | Easy to forget updating and difficult to summarize spending | R002 |
| Notes Application | Information becomes unorganized over time | R003 |
| Excel / Google Sheets | Manual data entry is time-consuming | R004, R008 |
| Memory | Users easily forget where money was spent | R001, R005 |

## 7. Design Implications

- The application should allow users to add expenses quickly with only a few steps.
- The system should automatically categorize expenses and display spending summaries on a dashboard.
- The interface should be simple, clean, and mobile-friendly.
- The team should avoid complicated forms or unnecessary features in the first MVP.
- The most important user need for the MVP is **simple and fast expense tracking with a clear spending summary**.
