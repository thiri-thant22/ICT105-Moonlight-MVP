# Customer Discovery Questions

## Project Title
Budget # Assumption-Evidence Table

| No. | Team Assumption | Evidence Collected | Evidence Type | Strength | Decision |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | People do not keep track of their daily expenses regularly. | 8 out of 10 interviewees said they usually rely on memory or only record expenses occasionally, making it difficult to remember where their money was spent. | Interview | Moderate | Keep |
| 2 | People often spend more money than they planned each month. | 7 out of 10 respondents said they exceeded their monthly budget because they did not monitor their spending regularly. | Interview | Moderate | Keep |
| 3 | People use simple methods instead of budgeting applications to manage their finances. | Most respondents said they use a notebook, phone notes, Excel, or simply remember their expenses rather than using a dedicated budgeting app. | Interview | Moderate | Keep |

## Evidence Strength Guide
- Assumption 1: shows that many users do not regularly track their expenses and often rely on memory, which causes difficulty in managing money.
- Assumption 2: proves that users frequently exceed their planned budget because they do not monitor their spending consistently.
- Assumption 3: indicates that most users still use simple methods such as notebooks, phone notes, or Excel instead of dedicated budgeting applications, showing the need for an easier budgeting solution.



## Target Respondent
1. University students
2. Young professionals

## Consent and Context
1. May we ask you a few questions for our ICT105 course project?
2. Your participation is voluntary. We will not collect unnecessary personal data. Is it okay if we take notes?

## Recent Experience
3. Tell us about the last time you spent more money than you planned.
4. What were you trying to buy or pay for at that time?
5. What happened from the beginning until you realized you had overspent?

## Current Workaround
6. How do you currently track or manage your daily expenses?
7. What tools, apps, notebooks, spreadsheets, or methods do you use to record your spending?
8. What is difficult, confusing, time-consuming, or frustrating about your current way of managing expenses?


## Pain Severity and Consequence
9. How often do you lose track of your spending or go over your budget?
10. On a scale of 1–5, how serious is this problem for you? Why?
11. What happens when you do not track your expenses or exceed your budget?


## Closing
12. Is there anything else we should understand about your experience managing personal expenses?
13. May we use your anonymized response as part of our course project evidence?
